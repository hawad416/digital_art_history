05: Object Type & Motif
Which garments carry floral motifs?
Heatmap of floral prevalence: darker purple = higher proportion of floral items in that country + garment combination. Empty = that garment type doesn't appear in that country's collection. Numbers show total items; percentage shows floral rate.
What this reveals

Kerchiefs and caps are the most floral garment types, particularly in Croatia's Posavina region. Stockings, aprons, and belts are almost entirely geometric across all regions. This suggests floral motifs were concentrated in face-framing and head accessories the most visible, identity-expressing parts of a costume, while structural garments default to geometric pattern-weaving.

06:Regional Position
Mapping each region on the floral–geometric axis
Each bubble is a country, positioned by its floral-only % (vertical) and geometric-only % (horizontal). Size = collection size. Regions in the bottom-right are dominantly geometric; top-left would be dominantly floral. Do any countries share a visual identity?
Contextualizing the scatter

No region is "floral dominant" and every country sits closer to the geometric axis. But the distance from the diagonal matters: Croatia and Macedonia have notably different floral rates despite similar geometric rates, which points to distinct regional aesthetic traditions. Bosnia-Herzegovina and Serbia cluster close together, suggesting shared visual conventions despite political separation.
