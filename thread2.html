Does garment type predict floral motif more strongly than country or gender?
Thread 2 investigates whether the function of a garment correlates with its motif. The hypothesis: garments worn close to the face or used as identity-expressing accessories (kerchiefs, caps) should concentrate floral motifs, while structural items (shoes, sashes) should skew geometric. The data largely supports this but with important regional caveats from Thread 1. Note: these visualizations draw on the broader dataset that includes the object type column, which was not available in the Tableau analysis.

What the object pattern tells us

The kerchief's 80% floral rate connects to what the Blanche Payne archive documents: women would often weave flowers or coins into their hair and headdresses marked age and marital status. Floral motifs concentrated in face-framing, visible accessories, not randomly distributed across all garment types. Shoes and sashes, by contrast, are structural items where geometric pattern-weaving dominates.

Floral rate by garment type × country Heatmap — darker = higher floral rate

Each cell shows the number of items and the floral inclusion rate for that garment type and country combination. Empty cells mean that garment type does not appear in that country's collection — itself a curatorial signal.

What the heatmap reveals

Croatia's kerchief column is the darkest in the table (92% floral), reflecting Posavina's dominance. Serbia's stocking cell is also notably dark (80%) — driven entirely by Sumadija men's ceremonial stockings. Macedonia and Serbia's apron cells are both present but much lighter than Croatia's, confirming that the same garment type carries different motif traditions across regions.

Does the kerchief pattern hold across regions? Kerchief floral rate by region

If garment type truly predicts motif regardless of region, the kerchief should be predominantly floral everywhere. This mostly holds — but with one significant exception in Macedonia.

The kerchief caveat

The overall 80% kerchief floral rate is heavily driven by Posavina's 20 kerchiefs at 90% floral. Macedonia's Debar and Pelagonija kerchiefs are 0% floral. Regional tradition can override garment-type tendency — which reinforces Thread 1's finding that region is the stronger predictor of motif.

The stocking anomaly Men's vs women's stockings by region

Overall stockings are 27% floral. But men's stockings (53% floral) are more floral than women's (20%) — inverting the gender hypothesis for this one garment type.

Why the stocking anomaly is a curatorial story

Serbia's Sumadija contributes 12 men's stockings — all 100% floral. These were ceremonial pieces, likely selected precisely because of their elaborate embroidery. This is the most direct example in this dataset of how Blanche Payne's selection criteria shaped our findings.

Methodology & exclusions

Floral inclusion rate = items where floral = yes divided by total items of that type. All calculations use the 500 classified rows. Garment types with n≥10 are shown in the main proportional bars. Items with n<10 are shown in the heatmap but should not be interpreted as cultural signals — they reflect collection gaps.
