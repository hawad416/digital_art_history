Citations
Primary Sources
Archives & Collections

Payne, Blanche. Blanche Payne Regional Costume Photograph and Drawing Collection. University of Washington Libraries, Special Collections. Accessed 2026. https://content.lib.washington.edu/payneweb/

Primary archive — photographs, drawings, and field notes from Payne's 1930 and 1936–37 Yugoslavia fieldwork.

Payne, Blanche. "Historical Background of Former Yugoslavia." Blanche Payne Regional Costume Photograph and Drawing Collection. University of Washington Libraries, Special Collections. Accessed 2026. https://content.lib.washington.edu/payneweb/history.html

Historical background section of the Payne archive — primary source for the quote on regional identity and national dress used throughout the main research.

Museum Collections

Henry Art Gallery. "Costume and Textiles." Henry Art Gallery Collections. University of Washington. Accessed 2026. https://henryart.org/collections/costume-textiles

Overview of the Henry Art Gallery's costume and textile holdings, including the history of the Costume and Textile Study Center and the Blanche Payne Collection.

Secondary Sources
Historical Reference

"Austro-Hungarian Rule in Bosnia and Herzegovina." Wikipedia. Wikimedia Foundation. Last modified June 6, 2026. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_rule_in_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina

Background on Habsburg administration of Bosnia-Herzegovina 1878–1918, including the promotion of Bosnian folklore and regional identity under Austro-Hungarian rule.

"Yugoslavia." 1914–1918-Online: International Encyclopedia of the First World War. Freie Universität Berlin. Accessed 2026. https://encyclopedia.1914-1918-online.net/article/yugoslavia/

Used for historical context on the formation of Yugoslavia, the Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes, and regional political history.

Folk Costume & Regional Dress

Jozanović, Nikolina. "The Most Valuable Hallmark of Zagreb." LoveZagreb.hr. Accessed 2026. https://www.lovezagreb.hr/topics/more-than-words/the-most-valuable-hallmark-of-zagreb/

Used to clarify the relationship between Zagreb and Šestine in Thread 1 — Zagreb's traditional folk costume is named after Šestine, the highland village on the city's outskirts where the tradition originated.
